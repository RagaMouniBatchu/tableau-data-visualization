1Cars: Horsepower vs. MPG

This scatter plot effectively captures the inverse relationship between a car's horsepower and its fuel efficiency (MPG). By mapping horsepower to the x-axis and MPG to the y-axis, a clear downward trend is visible, indicating that more powerful cars generally consume more fuel. The color coding by Origin helps distinguish regional design patterns, and using marker size to represent vehicle Weight adds a third dimension that reveals how heavier cars often fall on the lower-efficiency end of the spectrum.

2Cereals: Sugar Distribution by Shelf

This box-and-whisker plot reveals the distribution of sugar content across shelf placements in the cereal aisle. Shelf 3 contains several high-sugar cereals with a broader spread, while Shelf 1 tends to have cereals with lower sugar content. Each dot represents one cereal, making it easy to identify outliers and healthier options within each group.

3Films: How Has Average Movie Length Changed?

This line chart tracks the average length of movies from 1920 to 1995. It highlights the evolving preferences in filmmaking, where film durations initially fluctuated but gradually stabilized around 100 minutes. This trend suggests a balance between creative storytelling and audience attention spans over the decades.

4Grocery Stores: Which Chains Drive the Most Revenue?

This treemap visualizes total revenue per grocery chain based on customer purchase amounts. Each rectangle's size represents the cumulative sales volume for that chain, with darker shades indicating higher revenue. It clearly shows that Major A and Major B dominate consumer spending, while regional and smaller chains generate comparatively lower totals.

5Mutual Funds: Which Categories Perform Best Over Time?

This grouped bar chart compares returns across mutual fund categories for year-to-date (YTD), 5-year, and 10-year periods. It clearly shows how some categories, like Large Growth, offer strong long-term returns, while others, like Small Value, may perform better in shorter timeframes. This helps investors balance short-term gains with long-term stability.
